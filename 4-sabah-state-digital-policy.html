Sabah State Digital Policy

Sabah State Digital Policy JPANS/BKD/SDGI/004 Mei 2025

4.1 Digital Transformation Policy Statement

Sabah is committed to advancing its digital transformation journey through a structured and strategic approach under the Sabah Digital Government Innovation (SDGI) initiative. From Automation to Innovation, and then from Innovation to Intelligence, Sabah is transitioning towards a data-driven government that leverages technology, artificial intelligence, and open data to improve governance, enhance citizen experience, and drive economic growth.

The evolution of Sabah's digital strategy reflects a progressive shift in focus:

  • In the SDGI 2020–2024 phase, foundational infrastructure was built, including the development of the DIIP platform, Sabah Digital ID (Sabah ePass), Digital Sabah Portal, and the establishment of centralised data hubs (People, Business, and Geo Hub). These efforts laid the groundwork for a more connected and efficient government.
  • With the SDGI 2025–2030 roadmap, Sabah enters a new era focused on intelligence and data-driven decision-making. This includes scaling up the DIIP platform using X-Road technology, expanding the use of Sabah ePass as the standard digital identity solution, integrating artificial intelligence into at least 50% of government systems, and enabling open data-as-a-service for businesses and innovators.
The 5A Principle: The ultimate goal is to build a government that is efficient, transparent, responsive, and capable of delivering personalised services to all Sabahans — Accessible by Anyone at Anytime, Anywhere, and by using Any Device.

This policy aligns with national frameworks such as MyDIGITAL and the 13th Malaysia Plan (RMKe-13), ensuring that Sabah plays a vital role in Malaysia's broader digital economy aspirations. By embracing this dual-phase approach of foundation building followed by intelligent governance, Sabah is positioning itself as a regional leader in smart, sustainable, and inclusive digital transformation.

4.2 Core Principles

PrincipleDescription
Citizen-Centric Approach Prioritise the needs of citizens by providing accessible, efficient, and user-friendly digital services.
Data-Driven Governance Utilise data as a strategic asset to inform decision-making, improve resource allocation, and enhance public policy.
Innovation and Technology Embrace emerging technologies such as Artificial Intelligence (AI) and data analytics to drive innovation and improve service delivery.
Security and Trust Ensure the security, privacy, and integrity of data and digital systems.
Collaboration and Integration Foster collaboration among government agencies, the private sector, and the community to achieve shared digital goals.

4.3 Key Policy Objectives

Integrated Digital Ecosystem

Establish an integrated digital ecosystem that connects all government agencies, ensuring seamless data sharing and interoperability — including implementing the DIIP-X platform for secure data sharing and developing the Sabah Digital ID as the official digital identification providing a single point of access to government services.

Enhanced Public Service Delivery

  • Automate government processes to reduce manual tasks and bureaucratic delays
  • Provide digital access to 95% of main government services by 2030
  • Reduce government transaction processing time by 50% or more by 2030

Data-Driven Decision Making

  • Ensure that 100% of government data is available for AI processing by 2030
  • Employ data analytics to improve policy development, resource allocation, and programme implementation

AI and Innovation

  • Implement AI in at least 50% of government systems by 2030
  • Execute a minimum of 50 AI-based projects in the Sabah public sector
  • Promote the use of AI in public services for improved efficiency and effectiveness

Skilled Digital Workforce

  • Train at least 5,000 public servants in basic AI usage by 2030
  • Encourage talent development in data science, analytics, and AI

Digital Economy Growth

  • Support digital innovation and startups
  • Attract investments in digital infrastructure

Cybersecurity and Data Protection

  • Establish robust cybersecurity measures to protect government systems
  • Implement data governance policies aligned with national standards

Accessibility and Inclusion

  • Make digital government services available anytime, anywhere, on any device (5A Principles)

4.3 Roles of Stakeholders

4.3.1 Jabatan Perkhidmatan Awam Negeri Sabah (JPANS)

JPANS is the primary agency responsible for the overall implementation and execution of the Digital Government Programme and its various projects. Specifically, this includes:

  • Owning and leading the implementation of the Platform Integrasi DIIP-X and ID Digital Sabah project
  • Owning and leading the Inisiatif Kecerdasan Data Sabah (SDII)
  • Owning and leading the Transformasi Digital Perkhidmatan Awam Negeri (TDPAN)
  • Managing project planning, execution, and monitoring
  • Coordinating with other government agencies and stakeholders
  • Ensuring the achievement of project goals and Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

JPANS is central to driving the digital transformation of the state's public sector and ensuring the various digital initiatives are aligned with the overall objectives.

4.3.2 Agensi Perkhidmatan Awam Negeri Sabah (State Agencies)

These are the various government departments and agencies within Sabah that will be directly impacted by the digital transformation initiatives and are expected to adopt and use the new digital systems and platforms. Their specific roles include:

  • Adopting and integrating the DIIP-X platform into their existing systems
  • Utilising the Sabah Digital ID for citizen authentication and access to services
  • Implementing AI and data analytics into their operations
  • Contributing data to the integrated data ecosystem and data sharing
  • Participating in training and capacity-building programmes related to AI and data analytics
  • Transforming their services to be digitally accessible, including workflows and business processes
  • Adopting changes to achieve the targets set by the Digital Transformation Policy

These agencies are the key users and beneficiaries of the digital initiatives, and their active participation is crucial for successful implementation.

4.3.3 Rakyat Sabah (Sabah Residents)

Citizens are the end-users of the digital services and are expected to benefit directly from the improvements in government efficiency and service delivery. Their specific roles include:

  • Registering for and using the Sabah Digital ID to access government services
  • Engaging with government services through digital platforms
  • Providing feedback and participating in consultations to help improve services
  • Accessing services more easily, quickly, and securely

The initiatives aim to improve the lives of Sabah citizens by making government services more accessible and efficient. The success of the initiatives hinges on citizen adoption and use.

4.3.4 Organisasi Swasta (Private Sector Organisations)

The private sector is a key stakeholder that is expected to benefit from the digital transformation of government services. They will:

  • Utilise the DIIP-X platform for secure data sharing and application integration with government agencies
  • Use the Sabah Digital ID for business transactions and interactions with the government
  • Contribute to the growth of Sabah's digital economy through investments and innovation
  • Benefit from streamlined processes for business permits, approvals, and other government interactions

The private sector plays a vital role in driving economic growth and can greatly benefit from a more efficient and transparent government.

These stakeholders, working collaboratively, are crucial for the successful implementation of the Sabah Digital Government initiatives. The state government has clearly established the need for all stakeholders to participate in these initiatives to achieve their objectives.

4.4 Innovation and Experimentation Strategy

To ensure Sabah remains at the forefront of digital transformation, an innovation-driven approach must be embedded into all aspects of governance and service delivery. This requires creating a safe environment for testing new technologies, experimenting with novel approaches, and scaling what works effectively.

4.4.1 Key Components

Pilot Project Lifecycle Management

StageDescription
Idea Submission Agencies and external stakeholders can propose innovative ideas.
Feasibility Assessment Evaluate technical, financial, and operational feasibility.
Implementation Run small-scale pilots with measurable outcomes.
Evaluation Use KPIs and stakeholder feedback to assess impact.
Decision to Scale or Discontinue Based on results, decide whether to adopt, refine, or retire the initiative.

Collaboration with Academia and Private Sector

Partner with universities, research institutions, and local technology firms to co-develop prototypes and proof-of-concepts.

Knowledge Sharing Platform

  • Create a centralised repository of lessons learned, success stories, and case studies from past pilots
  • Host regular workshops and forums to share insights and promote cross-agency learning

4.4.2 Governance and Oversight

The Digital Government Division (DGD) will oversee the Innovation & Experimentation Strategy. A dedicated Innovation Unit may be established within DGD to manage and coordinate pilot projects, and report findings to the Working Committee.

By embedding an innovation-first culture into Sabah's digital governance framework, the state ensures that transformation is not a one-time event but a continuous, evolving process driven by data, experimentation, and shared learning.
