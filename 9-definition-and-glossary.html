9. Definition and Glossary

Rujukan: JPANS/BKD/SDGI/009 Versi 1.0 (Draf) SDGI Roadmap 2020–2030

Definitions

Artificial Intelligence (AI)
A branch of computer science that enables machines to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as learning, reasoning, problem-solving, and decision-making. In Sabah's digital government strategy, AI is used to enhance public service delivery, automate workflows, and support data-driven policymaking.
Blockchain
A decentralized digital ledger technology that records transactions or data in a secure, transparent, and tamper-proof manner. Blockchain can be used to ensure integrity and trust in digital systems, such as identity management, land registration, and secure data exchange.
Cybersecurity
The practice of protecting digital systems, networks, and data from cyber threats such as hacking, data breaches, and unauthorized access. Cybersecurity is essential for safeguarding citizen data, ensuring trust in digital services, and maintaining the integrity of government operations.
Data-As-A-Service (DaaS)
A model where data is made available to users or organizations in a structured, real-time, and standardized format — often through APIs. DaaS supports innovation, transparency, and efficient decision-making by enabling easy access to high-quality government data for businesses, researchers, and developers.
Data-driven
An approach that relies on the collection, analysis, and use of data to inform decisions, improve performance, and optimize outcomes. In Sabah's digital governance, being data-driven means using analytics and insights to shape policies, allocate resources, and deliver better public services.
Data Hub
A centralized system or platform where data from multiple sources is collected, stored, and made available for analysis and service delivery. In Sabah, there are three main Data Hubs — all integrated into the DIIP-X platform to enable seamless data sharing across agencies:
  • People Hub — Centralized repository of citizen data
  • Business Hub — Repository of business and enterprise-related data
  • Geo Hub — Geospatial data for urban planning and environmental monitoring
Digital Literacy
The ability to effectively use digital tools, platforms, and services to access information, communicate, and participate in the digital economy. Promoting digital literacy is crucial for ensuring inclusive access to digital government services and empowering citizens to benefit from Sabah's digital transformation.
E-Government (Electronic Government)
The use of digital technologies to deliver government services, engage citizens, and improve internal operations. E-government enhances efficiency, transparency, and accessibility by digitizing processes such as tax payments, license applications, and public consultations.

Geo Hub
A centralized geospatial data repository that provides location-based information for planning, monitoring, and decision-making. The Geo Hub integrates geographic data from various sources and is used for land administration, disaster management, environmental planning, and smart city development in Sabah.
Interoperability
The ability of different digital systems, platforms, and organizations to work together seamlessly and exchange data without barriers. Interoperability ensures that government agencies can share information efficiently, reducing duplication and improving service delivery.
People Hub
A centralized database containing citizen information used to provide personalized and efficient government services. The People Hub supports accurate identification, streamlined service delivery, and targeted policy implementation by consolidating citizen data from various departments.
Sabah Digital ID (Sabah ePass)
A secure digital identity solution that allows Sabah residents to authenticate themselves when accessing online government services. Sabah ePass serves as a single sign-on mechanism, ensuring secure and convenient access to e-services anytime, anywhere, and on any device.
X-Road
A secure and interoperable data exchange layer developed in Estonia that allows different information systems to share data seamlessly and securely. Sabah uses X-Road technology as part of its Data Integration and Interchange Platform (DIIP-X), enabling efficient and trusted data sharing between government agencies and external stakeholders.
5A Principle
A citizen-centric digital service design framework ensuring that services are accessible by anyone, at anytime, anywhere, and via any device. This principle underpins Sabah's digital transformation, ensuring inclusivity, convenience, and flexibility in accessing government services.
