Foreword by the Director of Digital Government Division

As the founding director of the Digital Government Division (DGD), reflecting on Sabah's digital journey fills me with a profound sense of pride and gratitude. When we first set out on this transformative path, we envisioned a future where every citizen, business, and public institution could access efficient, transparent, and inclusive digital services.

That vision took shape through the Sabah Digital Government Innovation (SDGI) Roadmap 2020–2024, which laid the groundwork for e-government, digital inclusion, and infrastructure expansion. Since then, Sabah has made tremendous strides in aligning technology with the aspirations of Sabah Maju Jaya, ensuring no community is left behind in the digital era.

The Sabah Digital Policy is a natural evolution of that vision. It articulates our principles and commitments to developing a digital ecosystem that is citizen-centric, secure, inclusive, and ethically governed. The policy outlines a strategic framework that supports digital infrastructure, data protection, service transformation, and the development of a digitally skilled workforce. More importantly, it empowers the state to leverage the digital economy for social good, institutional efficiency, and long-term economic growth.

Now extended through the SDGI Roadmap 2025–2030, this next phase boldly embraces the dawn of the Artificial Intelligence (AI) era. We aim to harness the transformative power of AI to improve governance, public services, and local industry competitiveness. This includes ethical AI adoption, AI-driven service delivery, and support for AI innovation within Sabah's digital ecosystem — all while safeguarding values of transparency, accountability, and equity.

As we enter this next chapter, my hope is that this policy will serve not only as a guide, but as an inspiration. Let it move each of us — policy-makers, civil servants, entrepreneurs, educators, and citizens — to the edges of what is possible. Let it spark a shared aspiration to shape a digital Sabah that is future-ready, inclusive, and resilient. The journey continues, and it belongs to all of us.

Doria Tai Yun Tyng

Director of Digital Government Division

From Innovation to Intelligence: Towards a Data-Driven Government

Sabah's digital transformation journey began from foundational automation and digitalisation efforts under the SDGI 2020–2024 to the next stage focusing on data-driven governance and intelligence under SDGI 2025–2030.

This shift marks a strategic move toward leveraging data as a strategic asset, embedding artificial intelligence (AI) in governance, and ensuring that decisions are informed by evidence, analytics, and real-time insights. The goal is not only to modernise systems but also to transform how the government operates, responds to citizen needs, and drives economic growth.

Key Shifts in Focus

  • From Automation to Data-Driven Governance: Moving beyond digitising processes to using data for predictive analysis, performance monitoring, and intelligent decision-making.
  • From Isolated Systems to Integrated Intelligence: Breaking down data silos through DIIP-X and X-Road, enabling seamless data sharing across agencies and with external stakeholders.
  • From Technology Adoption to Cultural Transformation: Building a culture of data literacy, innovation, and continuous improvement within the civil service and among citizens.

Strategic Objectives

This evolution supports the broader objectives of the Sabah Digital Policy, particularly in achieving:

  • A fully integrated digital ecosystem
  • AI-enabled public services
  • Transparent, accountable, and efficient governance
  • Inclusive and accessible digital services for all Sabahans
As Sabah transitions into this next phase, the focus will be on harnessing the power of data to improve lives, enhance service delivery, and position Sabah as a leader in smart and sustainable digital governance in Malaysia.

Sabah Digital Government Innovation (SDGI) — Evolution Timeline

Phase Period Focus Area Key Activities
Phase 1:
Foundation		 2020–2021 Building Infrastructure, Workflows & Framework
  • Development of DIIP (Data Integration & Interchange Platform)
  • Introduction of Sabah ePass (Digital ID) and Company Digital ID
  • Data Governance Maturity Index initiative
  • Launch of Digital Sabah Portal as single point of access
Phase 2:
Smart Workflows & Self-Service		 2021–2024 Standardisation, Governance & Data-as-a-Service
  • Establishment of People Hub, Business Hub, and Geo Hub
  • Sabah Open Data Initiative
  • AI pilot projects initiated
  • bizSABAH digital marketplace
  • X-Road integration with DIIP
Phase 3:
Towards a Data-Driven Government		 2025–2030 Intelligence, AI & Private Sector Collaboration
  • DIIP-X expanded to integrate with all government agencies
  • Sabah ePass becomes standard authentication for all e-services
  • AI integrated into at least 50% of government systems by 2030
  • Open Data-as-a-Service model launched for private sector reuse
  • Geo-Hub integrated into DIIP for land and environmental planning
