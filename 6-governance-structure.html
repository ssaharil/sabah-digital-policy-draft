6. Governance Structure

Rujukan: JPANS/BKD/SDGI/006 Versi 1.0 (Draf) SDGI Roadmap 2020–2030

6.1 Digital Government Division

The Digital Government Division, under the Sabah State Public Service Department, plays a crucial role in implementing this vision. One of the key initiatives is the Sabah Digital Government, guided by the 5A Principle: "Accessible, by Anyone, at Anytime, Anywhere, and by Any Device."

5A ElementDescription
AccessibleGovernment services should be easily accessible to all citizens through various digital channels.
AnyoneDigital government initiatives should cater to the needs of all citizens, regardless of their background, location, or digital literacy.
AnytimeServices should be available 24/7, allowing citizens to access them at their convenience.
AnywhereCitizens should be able to access government services from any location with an internet connection.
Any DeviceServices should be accessible through various devices, including smartphones, computers, and tablets.

6.2 Framework for Policy Implementation

For efficient implementation of Sabah Digital Policy, the framework includes the following governance tiers:

6.2.1 Main Committee

Responsibilities

  • Setting overarching goals and objectives for digital government initiatives.
  • Developing a comprehensive digital government strategy aligned with national agendas.
  • Approving policies, programs, and budgets related to digital government.
  • Monitoring overall progress and evaluating the effectiveness of initiatives.

Composition

  • Chaired by the State Secretary.
  • Representatives from key government agencies, including the Digital Government Division, Ministry of Finance, and Ministry of Education.
  • Experts in technology, innovation, and public administration.

6.2.2 Working Committee

Responsibilities

  • Developing detailed implementation plans for digital government policies.
  • Coordinating activities across different government agencies.
  • Overseeing the execution of programs and projects.
  • Monitoring progress, identifying challenges, and recommending solutions.
  • Reporting regularly to the Main Committee.

Composition

  • Technical experts and project managers.

6.2.3 Thrust Leaders

Responsibilities

  • Leading specific thrust areas within the digital government strategy (e.g., digital infrastructure, digital skills development, cybersecurity).
  • Developing and implementing action plans for their respective thrusts.
  • Coordinating with the Working Committee and providing updates on progress.

Selection

  • Individuals with expertise and leadership experience in the relevant thrust areas.

6.2.4 Monitoring and Evaluation

Process

  • Establish clear key performance indicators (KPIs) for each initiative.
  • Regularly track progress against KPIs.
  • Conduct periodic reviews and evaluations to assess effectiveness and identify areas for improvement.
  • Utilize data analytics to gain insights into the impact of digital government initiatives.

Tools

  • Performance dashboards.
  • Progress reports.
  • Surveys and feedback mechanisms.

6.3 Risk Management Strategy

Digital transformation introduces a wide range of risks that, if unmanaged, can delay progress, compromise data integrity, reduce public trust, and hinder service delivery. To ensure the successful implementation of Sabah's digital government initiatives, a robust Risk Management Strategy must be established. This strategy outlines the process for identifying, assessing, prioritizing, mitigating, and monitoring risks throughout the lifecycle of digital projects.

6.3.1 Risk Management Framework

The following five-phase framework will guide the identification and management of risks:

PhaseDescription
Phase 1: Risk IdentificationIdentify internal and external risks related to infrastructure, data, technology, human resources, cybersecurity, and stakeholder engagement.
Phase 2: Risk AssessmentEvaluate each identified risk in terms of its likelihood and impact.
Phase 3: Risk Mitigation PlanningFor high-priority risks, develop specific mitigation strategies including contingency planning, training, investment, or policy changes.
