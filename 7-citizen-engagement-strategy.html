7. Citizen Engagement Strategy

Rujukan: JPANS/BKD/SDGI/007 Versi 1.0 (Draf) SDGI Roadmap 2020–2030

7.1 Purpose

The success of Sabah's digital transformation depends not only on robust infrastructure and systems but also on the willingness and ability of citizens to adopt and utilize digital government services. This section outlines a comprehensive citizen engagement strategy aimed at building awareness, trust, and participation among Sabah's diverse population.

7.2 Objectives of Citizen Engagement

ObjectivesDescription
Raise AwarenessEducate citizens about available digital services and their benefits.
Build TrustEnsure transparency, security, and privacy to foster confidence in digital systems.
Promote AdoptionEncourage active use of digital tools such as Sabah Digital ID and online service portals.
Support InclusionEnsure underserved groups (e.g., rural communities, elderly, indigenous populations) are not left behind.
Enable FeedbackCreate channels for citizens to provide input and report issues with digital services.

7.3 Target Groups

Each group may require tailored communication and support strategies:

Urban vs. Rural Populations
Youth vs. Elderly

7.4 Key Strategies & Initiatives

7.4.1 Public Awareness Campaign

  • Launch multi-channel campaigns via social media and local influencers.
  • Use local dialects and culturally appropriate messaging.
  • Highlight real-life success stories (e.g., faster processing times, reduced travel).

7.4.2 Digital Literacy Programs

  • Collaborate with INSAN to deliver basic digital skills training.
  • Focus on using digitalization tools to access e-services.

7.4.3 Pilot Districts and Champions

  • Select pilot districts to demonstrate successful digital service delivery.
  • Use these zones to refine processes before scaling statewide.

7.5 Monitoring and Evaluation

Track metrics such as:

Number of Sabah Digital ID registrations Usage statistics of Digital Sabah's systems User satisfaction ratings

Adjust engagement strategies based on performance data.
